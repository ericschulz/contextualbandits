SPACE MINING

Please read the following instructions very carefully:


Welcome to SPACE MINING. You are a MINER working in outer space. It is your task to mine as many EMERALDS as you can from 4 different planets. Emeralds are important for mankind to produce energy for transportation and computers.

You will see 4 different planets: "PLANET 1", "PLANET 2", "PLANET 3", and "PLANET 4" and it will be your task to choose one planet where you want to mine for Emeralds on each trial. Because it is hard to mine Emeralds, you can only always mine one planet at a given round.

Additionally, you will also see 3 different environmental factors: "MECURY", "KRYPTON", and "NOBELIUM". These factors describe the galaxy at the given day when you are about to mine. Each of those factors can either be on, marked by a "+", or off, marked by a "-". The factors of the galaxy on a given day influence how many Emeralds you can mine from each planet.

It is therefore your task to learn how the different factors related to the potential number of Emeralds for each planet and then to choose the planet that you think will have the highest number of Emeralds.

Different planets might respond differently to the environmental factors of the galaxy. For example, "MERCURY" could be good for one Planet but bad for an other. It can also happen that a field produces Emeralds independently of how much "MERCURY" there is. It is your task to learn how the different PLANETS react to the environmental conditions and pick the best PLANET on each round accordingly.

You have a total amount of 150 trials available. That means you have 150 chances to click on a PLANET and mine the Emeralds of that planet. It is your task to choose the planet wisely, so that you first get a good feeling for planet is influenced by what environmental factor and how, but also to find the best planet for the given environmental factors as quickly as possible in order to maximize the number of Emeralds overall.

Even though the number of Emeralds are direclty related to the environmental factors, there is some variation in every round. This means that the mined number of Emeralds for each field can vary slightly and are not always the same. It is your task to determine how much they can vary. The way they vary is the same across all of the plabets, which means that you will not encounter big deviations for one planet and small deviations at others.

On the next page, you will see a short example. This example is designed so you get used to the structure of the task and does not count towards your performance assessment. The dependency of mined Emeralds in this example is very simple and not representative of the dependency in the actual task.
